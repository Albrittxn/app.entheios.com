Entheios AI puts qualified listing appointments directly on a real estate agent's calendar — homeowners who actually want to sell. We run the ads, an AI qualifies and books every lead, and the agent just shows up to close. The agent never touches lead gen, follow-up, or the chase.

Three tiers. Pick the one that fits the agent's volume.

# What's included

Every tier is the full pipeline, end to end:

- **Meta ads** — built, launched, and actively managed (monitored, tested, and optimized on an ongoing basis — not set-and-forget)
- **AI lead qualification** — every lead screened in under 60 seconds
- **Booking** — qualified prospects booked straight onto the agent's calendar
- **No-show recovery** — if a booked lead doesn't show, the AI follows up and works to rebook that same lead
- **CRM sync** — qualified prospects pushed into the agent's existing CRM
- **Weekly reporting**

# The guarantee

If we don't deliver the promised appointments within 90 days, the agent gets a full refund of the service fee — the money back in their account.

A **qualified appointment** is a legal homeowner who states intent to sell within six months and is inside the agent's service area.

Three things void the guarantee:

- **Ad spend drops below the tier minimum.** If they turn Meta off, we can't deliver.
- **They reject more than two qualified appointments.**
- **They no-show three or more booked meetings.**

No free trials, no payment plans, no "let me just try it for a month." The 90-day money-back guarantee is the risk-removal.

# Common questions

**Do the 10 / 20 appointments mean booked, or booked and shown up?**
Booked. An appointment counts toward the number the moment it's on the agent's calendar — whether or not the prospect shows up.

**Does the refund cover the ad spend too, or just the service fee?**
Just the service fee. The Meta ad spend isn't refundable — it was spent on real ads that ran.

**How does the agent pay for the Meta ad spend?**
Their choice, and they make it on the onboarding form. They can pre-pay the month up front, or put their own card on Meta and let Meta charge it directly (Meta bills daily, roughly $30–$35 a day).

**Are the ads actively managed, or set up once and left alone?**
Actively managed. We monitor, test, and optimize the campaigns on an ongoing basis — it is not set-and-forget.

**What happens when a booked lead no-shows?**
The no-show recovery kicks in and works to get that same lead rebooked onto the calendar. They aren't just written off.

**Which CRMs does the sync work with?**
Follow Up Boss, Command, kvCORE, and most others. Our system sits in front of the agent's CRM — it doesn't replace it. The agent keeps their existing software and workflow; we just push qualified prospects into it.

**What does it actually cost to get started?**
The service fee plus the first month of ad spend. Growth is $3,500 + $1,000. Scale is $7,500 + $2,000.

**How soon does it go live?**
Infrastructure is live around day five. Leads start hitting the calendar somewhere between day seven and day ten.

**Does the agent have to learn new software or switch CRMs?**
No. The system runs in front of whatever they already use — nothing to learn, no workflow change.

**Is there a free trial or a payment plan?**
No. We don't do trials or payment plans. The 90-day full-refund guarantee is the risk-removal instead — if a prospect won't move with their full fee on the line and a money-back promise behind it, they aren't a fit.
