# 1. Open the call

***Deck moment: Cover — "Stop chasing leads. Start closing deals."***

Open with intent. Walk in already assuming they're a client.

> *"Hey [Name], thanks for booking. Before I waste either of our time — what specifically pushed you to grab this call today?"*

That question does three things. It makes them say the pain out loud, it confirms this isn't a tire-kick, and it hands you the rest of the call's script on a plate. Whatever they say next, that's what you sell to.

Match their energy and push it ten percent harder. If they're casual, you're warm-casual. If they're clipped, you're efficient. Never be the lowest-energy person on the call.

# 2. Surface the pain

***Deck moment: Problem — 78% of deals go to the first agent to respond. 5-minute response window. 47-minute average text back.***

Every agent you talk to is bleeding deals. They know it. They feel it every Sunday night when the week restarts and nothing closed. Your job isn't to tell them — it's to make them quantify it.

### Map the archetype fast

- **Time-starved** — can't keep up with follow-up, lives in airplane mode
- **Scaling** — growing past referrals, pipeline goes cold between buyers
- **Burned** — past agency sold them a list, took their money, delivered zero
- **New** — no pipeline yet, burning savings to stay in the game
- **Team escapee** — just broke off a team, lost the lead flow overnight
- **Big producer** — closing at volume, capped because they're doing everything themselves

### Quantify the bleed

Hours per week. Deals lost to the agent who answered first. Dollars wasted on Zillow leads that went nowhere. Make them say the number out loud.

> *"If nothing changes in the next 90 days, what does that actually cost you — in deals, in hours, in income?"*

Let the silence sit. The number they give you is the number you anchor against at pricing.

If they tell you they spend nothing on marketing and aren't looking to invest — that's your exit. Kill it clean. Move on.

***Deck moment: 60-Second Demo.***

Use the demo slide as a breath. Don't talk over it — let them watch the AI qualify and book a meeting in real time. When it lands, transition with: *"That's not a video. That's our system, running right now. Let me show you how it gets to your calendar."*

# 3. Show how it works

***Deck moment: How It Works → What You Get.***

This is where closers lose deals by talking about the product instead of the result. The prospect does not care about AI workflows, dialers, or SMS infrastructure. They care about what their Tuesday looks like in a month.

### Paint Month 2

Describe it like it's already happening.

> *"By week three you're showing up to calls with people who already know your price range, your area, and that they want to sell inside six months. Your job is the conversation. Not the chase, not the follow-up, not the three-week nurture. You walk into the meeting and you close the meeting."*

### Walk What You Get

Let the slide do the heavy lifting — don't read it. Land the headline: *"Everything from the ad to the appointment is ours. The CRM, the AI, the booking, the no-show recovery, the weekly reporting. You get the meeting. We do the rest."*

### Handle the CRM question before they ask it

This is the unlock. Tell them the system sits in front of their existing CRM.

> *"We're not replacing your Follow Up Boss, your Command, your kvCORE. Our system lives in front of it. We catch the lead, qualify it, book the meeting, and push the qualified prospect into whatever you're already using. You don't learn new software. You don't change your workflow. You just start getting meetings on your calendar."*

That line closes half the technical objections before they're raised.

# 4. Why we're different

***Deck moment: The Difference — they sell lists, we deliver appointments.***

If they've been burned before, this is where you rebuild trust. If they haven't, this is where you inoculate them against every other pitch they'll hear this week.

> *"Most agencies sell you a list of names. They take five grand, hand you 500 cold leads, and say good luck. We don't sell leads. We deliver meetings — on your calendar, qualified, with someone who actually wants to sell their house in the next six months. If we don't deliver, you don't pay."*

Keep it short. Don't oversell the difference — the guarantee finishes that work for you two slides later.

# 5. Present the pricing

***Deck moment: Pricing Branch — three tiers shown side-by-side. Growth, Scale, Enterprise.***

Do not pitch a tier. Let them pick.

When the pricing slide hits, ask:

> *"Looking at these three, which one do you think you best fall into?"*

This does more work than any pitch you could run. When a prospect picks their own tier, three things happen at once:

1. They stop being a prospect being sold to. They become a buyer making a decision.
2. Their pick tells you exactly how they see themselves. Solo agents pick Growth. Producers pick Scale. Ego picks higher than reality.
3. You get to confirm, push up, or push down — and either direction feels collaborative, not manipulative.

### The tiers

**Growth — $3,500.** Solo agents scaling past referrals. $1,000/mo minimum to Meta. Ten qualified appointments in 90 days.

**Scale — $7,500.** Small teams and high-volume producers. $2,000/mo minimum to Meta. Twenty qualified appointments in 90 days.

**Enterprise — custom.** Large teams and luxury. Quoted after this call if it fits.

***Deck moment: Tier Deep Dive — click into the tier they picked.***

### Reading their pick

- **Picks Growth, is clearly a producer** → push up. *"Honestly, based on what you just told me about your volume, Scale is the one that makes sense. Growth caps you at ten meetings and you'd burn through those in three weeks."*
- **Picks Scale, is actually solo** → confirm but reality-check. *"Scale works. The only question is whether you've got the capacity to handle twenty meetings a quarter on top of everything else you're running."*
- **Picks Enterprise** → treat it as a qualification signal. Slow down, book a second call with pricing TBD.

### Anchor against the deal

Bring back the number they gave you in Section 2. One missed commission is $10K+. The entire Growth tier is less than a third of that — and they get it back in full if we miss the number.

# 6. The guarantee

***Deck moment: Guarantee — "If we fail to deliver, you get a full refund."***

This is the simplest part of the call and closers still find ways to screw it up by over-explaining. Don't.

> *"If we don't deliver the appointments inside 90 days, you get a full refund of the service fee. Not store credit. Not more time. The money back in your account."*

### Three things void it

Don't read these like a legal disclaimer. Frame them as "the ways this only works."

- **Ad spend stays at the tier minimum.** If they turn off Meta, we can't deliver.
- **They don't reject qualified appointments.** A qualified appointment is a legal homeowner, stating intent to sell within six months, inside their service area. Reject more than two and the guarantee is off.
- **They show up.** No-show three or more booked meetings and the guarantee is off.
- **No free trials, payment plans, or "what if I just try it for a month."** We don't do them. Ever. The 90-day full-refund guarantee is the risk-removal — if a prospect won't move with their full fee on the line and a money-back promise behind it, they're not a fit.

That's it. Move on.

# 7. Close the deal

***Deck moment: Next Steps.***

Ask the question. Then shut up.

> *"Sounds like this is exactly what you need. Want to get started today?"*

First to speak loses.

### When they say yes

- Click "Start Onboarding" on the Tier Deep Dive slide → Fanbasis checkout opens
- Stay on screen share, walk them through entering payment
- Stay on the line until payment clears
- Confirm the onboarding email landed before they hang up
- Tell them exactly what happens next

> "Payment just cleared. Onboarding email is on its way — you'll see a service agreement and a quick form. Fill it out tonight, we start building tomorrow. Infrastructure is live on day five. Leads start hitting your calendar somewhere between day seven and day ten."
