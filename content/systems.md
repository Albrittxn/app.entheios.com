# 1. Getting Calls

Prospects book a call with us through one of two booking forms — a longer **inbound** form (agents who found us through our ads) and a shorter **outbound** form (agents we reached out to directly). Either way it works the same:

1. The call gets assigned to a closer — that's you when it's your turn.
2. It shows up automatically in your **Closing → Calls** tab, and it's added to your Google Calendar with a link to join the video call.

The Closing hub is closers-only — if you can see it, you're in the rotation. In **Calls** you see just your own calls, split into **Upcoming** and **Past**. Click any row to open the full lead info and your after-call form.

If a prospect reschedules or cancels, the row updates on its own — a rescheduled call gets flagged, a cancelled one is marked `Cancelled`. You never have to fix it by hand.

# 2. Pre Call

**One-time setup.** Bookmark [slides.entheios.com](http://slides.entheios.com). The deck is hosted and always current — you just open the tab before each call, no download, no re-bookmark when it updates.

**~5 minutes before each call**, open the booking's row in **Calls**. The left side is read-only context, pulled straight from what the prospect submitted when they booked:

- **Inbound leads** show the full picture — brokerage, market, annual closings, average sale price, their biggest bottleneck, and a free-text **Prospect notes** quote.
- **Outbound leads** are lighter — brokerage, state, and their **Outreach notes**.

Read it and pick one specific hook to open with — high listing volume, a recent brokerage move, something in their notes.

Then join from the video link. You'll find it on the call row, in your Cal booking, and on your Google Calendar event. Every call runs in your browser — there's nothing to download or install, and the call records on its own.

Have your bookmarked deck tab open and ready to share.

# 3. During Call

**The deck flow**

Share your screen. Walk through Cover → Problem → 60-Second Demo → How It Works → What You Get → The Difference → Pricing Branch. On Pricing Branch, ask: *"Looking at these three, which one do you think you best fall into?"* They pick → click into Tier Deep Dive for the tier they picked → walk the cost and guarantee for that tier.

**Closing — Growth or Scale**

On the Tier Deep Dive slide, click the **"Start Onboarding"** button. This opens the Fanbasis checkout for that tier. Stay on screen share. Stay on the line. Enter their payment details on-call. Payment confirmation auto-triggers the onboarding form to their email — confirm they received it before you end the call.

**Closing — Enterprise**

The "Start Onboarding" button on the Enterprise tier opens a form. Fill it out together on screen share, then book a follow-up call 24+ hours out — custom pricing gets presented on that call, not this one. No payment on the first Enterprise call.

# 4. Post Call

**Within 30 minutes — and always before your next call.** Open the row in **Calls** and fill out the right-side form:

- **Status** — `Booked`, `Cancelled`, `No-Show`, `Closed`, `No Close`, `Follow-Up Requested`, or `Nurture`.
- **Objections** — multi-select everything that came up.
- **Follow-up date** — appears once you set Status to `Follow-Up Requested`; put in the date you intend to circle back.
- **Notes** — anything the dropdowns can't capture: what they pushed back on, what landed, what the team should know going into a follow-up or escalation.

Hit **Save**. That's it — you never upload a recording. The **Recording** and **Transcript** links appear on the left side of the row on their own, about 10 minutes after the call ends.

# 5. No-Show Process

If the prospect isn't on the call 5 minutes past the start time:

1. Open their row, use the one-click copy on the **Phone** field, and call them directly from your phone.
2. No answer → set **Status = No-Show** and Save.
3. Don't send your own text. No-show follow-up is handled separately — a message from you on top of it only confuses the prospect.
